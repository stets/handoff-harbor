Acceptance is not just approval. It is a boundary.

A final acceptance message gives the client one last clean review point before the project moves from build mode into support mode. It protects both sides: the client can confirm the important pieces, and you can stop treating every new idea as part of the original scope.

The goal is not to sound legalistic. The goal is to make the transition obvious: delivered items are accepted, known issues are listed, final invoice terms are clear, and future edits go through the right channel.

What to confirm before marking the project accepted

Deliverables

List the pages, templates, CMS setup, forms, integrations, analytics, redirects, training, and handoff files included in the project.

Client review

Give the client a short deadline to review priority flows, content, forms, mobile views, and any launch-critical details.

Known issues

Separate true launch blockers from nice-to-have requests, third-party limitations, future content work, and post-launch improvements.

Payment and support

State what final payment unlocks, when included bug support ends, and how new requests move into a paid change request or care plan.

Copy/paste client acceptance checklist

  • ☐ Final site URL, staging URL, and launch date are listed.
  • ☐ Delivered pages/templates/features are written in plain language.
  • ☐ Client has reviewed priority pages on desktop and mobile.
  • ☐ Forms, checkout, booking, signup, newsletter, and contact flows have been tested or marked not applicable.
  • ☐ Admin/login access, ownership, billing, DNS, hosting, analytics, and third-party tools have been handed off or documented.
  • ☐ Known issues are split into launch blockers, accepted limitations, and future improvements.
  • ☐ Final invoice/payment status is stated clearly.
  • ☐ Included support window, response expectations, and excluded work are documented.
  • ☐ New work after acceptance routes to a change request, support request, or care-plan agreement.
  • ☐ Client acceptance/signoff is saved with the project records.

Short acceptance email

Subject: Website acceptance + final handoff Hey [Name] — the site is ready for final acceptance. I included a short acceptance checklist below so we have a clean record of what was delivered, what you reviewed, and what moves into post-launch support or future change requests. Please review the priority pages, mobile views, and main forms by [date]. If there are no launch-blocking issues, I will treat the project as accepted and move it into the handoff/support window listed below. Included launch support runs through [date]. New feature requests, content additions, redesign work, third-party tool changes, or items outside the accepted scope can be handled as a separate change request or care-plan item.
