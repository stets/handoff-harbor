Framer handoffs break down when the site is polished but the operating details are vague.

Framer makes beautiful marketing sites fast, but the post-launch mess is familiar: who owns the workspace, who pays the plan, where domain/DNS access lives, which forms and integrations are connected, how CMS edits should be handled, and whether layout or animation changes are included after acceptance.

This is not legal, tax, or platform policy advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for freelance designers and small studios so a Framer launch ends with ownership, access, and support expectations in writing.

What to document before a Framer project is accepted

Workspace, plan, and billing ownership

Confirm the client owns or has accepted the right Framer workspace/site role, billing owner, renewal date, recovery email, 2FA expectations, and agency access.

Domains, DNS, redirects, and launch settings

Record registrar ownership, connected domains, DNS records, SSL status, www/non-www preference, redirects, custom code, favicon, sitemap, and publish workflow.

Forms, CMS, analytics, and integrations

List form destinations, CRM/newsletter routes, CMS collection ownership, analytics, pixels, Search Console, embeds, third-party scripts, API keys, and renewal costs.

Editing and support boundaries

Separate safe text/CMS edits from paid work: new sections, responsive fixes, animation/layout changes, CMS rebuilds, SEO updates, integrations, forms, and troubleshooting.

Copy/paste Framer handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client controls or has accepted the correct Framer workspace/site ownership, plan, billing responsibility, recovery email, and 2FA expectations.
  • ☐ Editor roles, agency access, collaborators, old contractors, and unnecessary permissions have been reviewed and cleaned up.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, connected domains, DNS records, SSL status, redirects, email records, and renewal dates are documented.
  • ☐ Forms are tested; notification recipients, CRM/newsletter destinations, spam handling, and follow-up automations are documented.
  • ☐ CMS collections, editable fields, collection-item limits, image/content rules, and client-safe editing notes are written down.
  • ☐ Analytics, Search Console, pixels, SEO basics, sitemap/robots status, custom code, embeds, and conversion checks are transferred or documented.
  • ☐ Responsive breakpoints, menus, buttons, forms, key animations, and important browser/device checks are complete.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, performance notes, unsupported features, and future recommendations are captured.
  • ☐ Included support window is stated, and new sections, redesigns, animation/layout changes, CMS changes, SEO, integrations, or troubleshooting routes to paid support.

Short Framer handoff email

Subject: Framer site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the Framer site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the operating pieces are clear: workspace/site ownership, plan billing, domains/DNS, redirects, forms, CMS collections, analytics, integrations, editor access, responsive checks, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New sections, redesigns, animation/layout changes, CMS changes, SEO work, integrations, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
