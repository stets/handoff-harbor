Training is part of the handoff, not forever support

A clean training session gives the client confidence without turning you into their unpaid help desk. The trick is to teach the recurring tasks, skip edge-case rabbit holes, and write down where the handoff ends.

Use this checklist before your final call or include it inside the client handoff packet.

What to cover in the training call

Login and roles

Show the correct login URL, explain user roles, confirm who owns admin access, and remove temporary contractor accounts after handoff.

Editing routine content

Teach only the tasks the client will repeat: editing pages, posts, events, menu labels, images, team profiles, or product/service blurbs.

Forms and lead flow

Show where form submissions go, how notifications work, what to do if a form stops delivering, and who owns the connected inbox or CRM.

Boundaries and next steps

Explain what is included in the warranty period, what counts as a new request, and how maintenance or hourly support is handled after launch.

Quick checklist

  • [ ] Training call has a written agenda
  • [ ] Session is recorded and linked in the handoff packet
  • [ ] Client can log in with their own account
  • [ ] Client knows how to make the 3-5 updates they actually need
  • [ ] Client knows what not to edit
  • [ ] Form/contact flow has been tested during or before training
  • [ ] Warranty/support boundary is explained before the call ends
  • [ ] Paid maintenance or support option is presented as the next step
