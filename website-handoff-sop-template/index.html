The SOP should protect your margin, not just organize files.

A website handoff SOP is the checklist your future self follows when the project is basically done but not truly closed. It makes sure the client approves the work, pays the final invoice, receives the right access, understands support limits, and knows how to pay you for future updates.

Use this as a lightweight internal process for WordPress, Webflow, Squarespace, Shopify, or custom brochure-site projects.

Copy/paste handoff SOP

  1. Final QA: Test forms, mobile layouts, analytics, redirects, page titles, backups, performance basics, and launch-critical pages.
  2. Client review: Send one review link with a deadline and ask for one consolidated change list tied to the agreed scope.
  3. Final invoice: Send the remaining balance before full access transfer or final handoff packet delivery.
  4. Handoff packet: Collect admin URLs, ownership notes, recurring costs, documentation links, training recording, and launch/support details.
  5. Access inventory: Confirm who owns domain, hosting, CMS, DNS, analytics, forms, email tools, plugins, licenses, and payment tools.
  6. Client training: Show the 3-5 routine edits they should know and explicitly name the areas they should not touch.
  7. Warranty window: State what bug fixes are included, the end date, and where requests should be sent.
  8. Maintenance next step: Offer a simple support plan or hourly path for new requests after handoff.
  9. Archive: Save final files, screenshots, credentials notes, invoice status, approvals, and handoff emails in the project folder.

What most handoffs miss

Payment tied to handoff

Do not deliver every login and file while the final invoice is still vague. Make payment and handoff part of the same closeout step.

Ownership clarity

Clients should know which accounts they own, which accounts you created, and which subscriptions will renew after launch.

Support boundaries

Write the difference between warranty fixes, new feature requests, maintenance, and emergency support before the first “quick tweak.”

Maintenance offer

The handoff is the cleanest moment to explain how ongoing updates work. Keep it useful, short, and specific.

Short internal SOP note

Project is ready for closeout when: [ ] QA checklist is complete [ ] Client has final review link + deadline [ ] Final invoice is sent [ ] Handoff packet is drafted [ ] Access inventory is complete [ ] Training/doc links are included [ ] Warranty window is written [ ] Maintenance/support path is offered [ ] Final approval + payment + handoff emails are archived
