Accessibility handoff keeps the conversation honest.

Most freelance projects do not include a full WCAG audit, legal review, remediation retainer, and assistive-technology testing. That is fine — if the client understands what was checked, what was not checked, and what future accessibility work costs.

This page is not legal advice or a compliance guarantee. It is a practical closeout checklist for designers who want to catch obvious issues and document scope clearly.

What to check before final signoff

Structure and headings

Confirm pages use one clear H1, sensible heading order, descriptive page titles, useful link text, and content that still makes sense without the visual layout.

Images and media

Add useful alt text for meaningful images, hide decorative images from screen readers where appropriate, and document any client-owned images that still need better descriptions.

Forms and interactive elements

Check labels, required fields, error messages, buttons, menus, accordions, popups, and embeds so the most important conversion paths are usable.

Keyboard, contrast, and focus

Tab through key pages, watch the focus state, test menus/modals, review color contrast, and record any third-party widget limits outside your control.

Copy/paste accessibility handoff checklist

  • ☐ Priority pages have clear titles, one main H1, and logical heading order.
  • ☐ Navigation links, buttons, and CTA text are descriptive without relying only on surrounding copy.
  • ☐ Meaningful images have useful alt text; decorative images are handled appropriately.
  • ☐ Color contrast has been checked for body text, buttons, labels, and important UI states.
  • ☐ Forms have visible labels, required-field notes, error states, success states, and correct destinations.
  • ☐ Main pages and critical flows can be navigated with a keyboard.
  • ☐ Focus states are visible on links, buttons, menus, and form fields.
  • ☐ Popups, cookie banners, chat widgets, booking embeds, maps, and other third-party tools are listed with known limits.
  • ☐ Video/audio content has captions, transcript notes, or a documented client follow-up item.
  • ☐ Accessibility scope is written into the final handoff so basic QA is not confused with a full compliance audit.
