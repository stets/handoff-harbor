Shopify handoff has more moving parts than a brochure site.

On a normal marketing site, a messy handoff usually means a few annoying edits. On Shopify, a messy handoff can mean payment issues, fulfillment confusion, app billing surprises, broken notifications, bad tax settings, or a client who still thinks every store question is included in the original build.

This is not legal, tax, or accounting advice. It is a practical project-closeout checklist for freelance designers and small studios so the client knows what they own, what they need to verify with their accountant or operations team, and what becomes paid support after launch.

What to document before the store is accepted

Ownership and access

Confirm the client owns the Shopify store, domain, payment accounts, email sender, analytics, theme licenses, and app billing relationships.

Money settings

Have the client verify payment providers, payout bank details, taxes, shipping rates, discount codes, gift cards, and refund/return policies.

Store operations

Document products, collections, inventory rules, order notifications, fulfillment flow, customer emails, staff permissions, and backup/export expectations.

Support boundaries

Separate launch bugs from new products, marketing changes, app configuration, inventory work, custom features, and ongoing store management.

Copy/paste Shopify handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client is the store owner or has accepted transfer of ownership.
  • ☐ Shopify plan, billing owner, billing email, and payment method are client-controlled.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, DNS records, SSL status, and primary domain settings are documented.
  • ☐ Shopify Payments/PayPal/Shop Pay/third-party gateways are connected and client-verified.
  • ☐ Payout bank details, tax settings, tax-region assumptions, and accounting review needs are clearly marked as client responsibilities.
  • ☐ Shipping zones, rates, pickup/local delivery options, fulfillment locations, and carrier/app dependencies are documented.
  • ☐ Products, variants, inventory tracking, collections, tags, redirects, policies, and customer notification templates have been reviewed.
  • ☐ Installed apps, app owners, monthly costs, trial end dates, renewal dates, and removal risks are listed.
  • ☐ Theme name, customizations, unpublished backup theme, license notes, and update limitations are recorded.
  • ☐ Test order, refund, discount code, abandoned cart, contact form, newsletter signup, and mobile checkout checks are complete.
  • ☐ Analytics, pixels, Search Console, product feeds, and email/SMS marketing destinations are owned or documented.
  • ☐ Included post-launch support window is stated, and store operations/new feature work routes to a paid support or change-request process.
