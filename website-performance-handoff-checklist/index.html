Website performance handoff checklist

A fast site at launch can get slow six weeks later when the client uploads giant images, installs plugins, changes hosting, or adds third-party scripts. Document the performance baseline before final signoff so speed issues do not turn into unpaid support.

Performance handoff protects both sides.

Clients remember “the website feels slow,” not whether the issue came from a 12MB hero image, a cheap host, a new chat widget, or thirty uncompressed gallery uploads after launch.

A simple performance record gives you a clean baseline: what was tested, what passed, what depends on client behavior, and what becomes paid optimization later.

What to check before final signoff

Baseline speed tests

Run at least one mobile and desktop test with PageSpeed Insights, Lighthouse, WebPageTest, or your preferred tool. Save the date, URLs, scores, and major warnings.

Images and media

Confirm hero images, galleries, logos, background videos, and uploaded assets are compressed, sized correctly, lazy-loaded where appropriate, and documented for the client.

Hosting and caching

Record the host, CDN, cache plugin/settings, SSL status, DNS notes, renewal owner, and any limitations that could affect load time after launch.

Third-party scripts

List analytics, pixels, booking embeds, chat widgets, reviews, maps, forms, and marketing scripts so future slowdowns have an obvious place to start.

Copy/paste performance handoff checklist

  • ☐ Home page and at least 2-3 priority pages were tested on mobile and desktop.
  • ☐ Test date, tool, scores, URLs, and major warnings are saved in the handoff packet.
  • ☐ Large images, galleries, logos, and background media were compressed and checked.
  • ☐ Lazy loading, responsive image sizing, font loading, and obvious render-blocking issues were reviewed.
  • ☐ Hosting, CDN, caching, SSL, DNS, and plugin/app dependencies are documented.
  • ☐ Third-party scripts and embeds are listed with owner, purpose, and removal risk.
  • ☐ Mobile page layout was checked on real devices or a documented browser/device matrix.
  • ☐ Client knows that future uploads, plugins, scripts, and hosting changes can affect speed.
  • ☐ Known performance limits are written down instead of hidden in a Slack thread.
  • ☐ Post-launch optimization is scoped as a care-plan item or separate paid work.

Performance baseline record template

Website performance handoff record Website: [client URL] Launch date: [date] Prepared by: [name] Pages tested: - [URL] — mobile score/notes: [score + issues] - [URL] — desktop score/notes: [score + issues] - [URL] — mobile/desktop notes: [score + issues] Media checks: - Hero images: [compressed / needs client follow-up] - Gallery images: [compressed / client-owned] - Video/background media: [notes] Hosting/caching: - Host/CDN: [provider] - Cache layer/plugin: [name/settings] - SSL/DNS owner: [client / agency / other] Third-party scripts: - Analytics/pixels: [tools] - Forms/booking/chat/maps/reviews: [tools] Known limits: - [cheap hosting, heavy app, client-owned image library, required scripts, etc.] Support boundary: - Launch-blocking speed bugs included until: [date] - Future performance optimization is: [care plan / quoted separately]
