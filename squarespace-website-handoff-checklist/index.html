Squarespace handoff gets messy when billing and ownership are vague.

Squarespace projects look simple until the client asks who owns the plan, who renews the domain, where form submissions go, why scheduling or email campaigns cost extra, and whether future content edits are included. If the handoff is casual, you become the unpaid translator for every platform setting.

This is not legal, accounting, or platform policy advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for freelance designers and small studios so clients know what they own, what they can edit, and what counts as paid support after launch.

What to document before a Squarespace project is accepted

Ownership and billing

Confirm the client controls the Squarespace site owner account, subscription plan, billing email, domain renewals, connected payment methods, and recovery access.

Domains, email, and forms

List domain registrar/DNS status, Google Workspace or email routing, form storage, notification recipients, newsletter destinations, and spam handling.

Platform features

Document scheduling, commerce, donations, member areas, email campaigns, third-party embeds, custom CSS/code injection, and any feature-specific costs.

Support boundaries

Separate launch bugs from ongoing content edits, SEO work, email campaign setup, commerce support, domain/email troubleshooting, new sections, and care-plan work.

Copy/paste Squarespace handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client is listed as site owner or has the correct owner/admin permissions.
  • ☐ Subscription plan, billing owner, renewal date, domain renewals, and paid add-ons are documented.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, nameservers, DNS records, SSL status, email routing, and renewal responsibility are recorded.
  • ☐ Contributors/users are cleaned up and client recovery email/2FA expectations are clear.
  • ☐ Forms are tested; storage destination, email notifications, CRM/newsletter destinations, and spam settings are documented.
  • ☐ Scheduling, commerce, donations, member areas, email campaigns, and other Squarespace features are tested or marked not in scope.
  • ☐ Payment processors, tax/shipping settings, product/catalog ownership, and order-notification routing are documented if commerce is enabled.
  • ☐ Analytics, Search Console, pixels, cookie/banner settings, and conversion checks are transferred or documented.
  • ☐ Client editing notes cover pages, navigation, blog posts, images, sections, buttons, forms, and reusable style limits.
  • ☐ Custom CSS/code injection, third-party embeds, scripts, and known risks are listed.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, future recommendations, and client signoff are recorded.
  • ☐ Included launch support window is stated, and new content, SEO, commerce, email, domain, and feature work route to paid support.

Short Squarespace handoff email

Subject: Squarespace site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the Squarespace site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the ownership and operating pieces are clear: Squarespace owner/admin access, billing, domain/DNS, forms, scheduling/commerce/email features, analytics, editing notes, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New pages, content edits, SEO work, email campaign setup, commerce changes, domain/email troubleshooting, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
