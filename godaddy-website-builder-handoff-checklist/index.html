A GoDaddy site is not handed off just because the client can log in.

The messy version: the freelancer built the site inside the wrong GoDaddy account, the domain and website plan renew on separate cards, forms go to the wrong inbox, bookings/payments are half-configured, and the client assumes every future website edit is still included.

This is not legal, tax, registrar, payment, or GoDaddy policy advice. It is a practical project-closeout checklist for freelancers and small studios so ownership, billing, editing limits, launch acceptance, and support boundaries are written down before the project disappears into unpaid maintenance.

What to document before a GoDaddy Website Builder project is accepted

Account, domain, and billing ownership

Confirm which GoDaddy account owns the site, domain, website plan, email, SSL, renewals, payment method, recovery email, delegated access, and who can remove the freelancer after handoff.

Forms, bookings, store, and notifications

Test contact forms, appointment settings, store products, checkout/payment connections, notification recipients, autoresponders, spam handling, and any third-party embeds or widgets.

DNS, email, analytics, and SEO basics

Record DNS records, branded email/Microsoft 365 status, redirects, metadata, Google Business Profile links, analytics/pixels, Search Console, cookie/privacy tools, and launch verification.

Client editing notes and support boundary

Explain safe text/image/section edits, risky settings, what the client owns after launch, the included bug-fix window, excluded new work, and paid maintenance or change-request options.

Copy/paste GoDaddy Website Builder handoff checklist

  • ☐ GoDaddy account owner, delegated access, recovery email, 2FA method, client users, and freelancer removal plan are confirmed.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, DNS records, SSL, website plan, email/Microsoft 365, renewal dates, payment method owner, and cancellation risk are documented.
  • ☐ Website pages, navigation, mobile view, footer, contact details, legal/privacy pages, and business hours are reviewed by the client.
  • ☐ Forms, notification emails, autoresponders, spam settings, booking/appointment flows, store/checkout/payment settings, and test submissions/orders are verified.
  • ☐ SEO titles/descriptions, social share image, redirects, Google Business Profile links, analytics/pixels, Search Console, and cookie/privacy tools are captured.
  • ☐ Any third-party embeds, reviews widgets, calendars, maps, chat tools, CRM/newsletter links, or tracking snippets are listed with ownership and login notes.
  • ☐ Client editing notes explain safe content/image edits, risky billing/domain/DNS/form/payment settings, image sizing, backups/export limitations, and when to ask for help.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, future recommendations, and unsupported requests are written down before final acceptance.
  • ☐ Included support window is stated, and new pages, copy changes, SEO, store changes, booking setup, DNS/email troubleshooting, or ongoing maintenance route to paid work.

Short GoDaddy handoff email

Subject: GoDaddy site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the GoDaddy site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the operating pieces are clear: GoDaddy account ownership, domain/DNS/SSL, website plan and renewals, forms, bookings/store settings if used, email, analytics/SEO, editing notes, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New pages, copy edits, SEO work, store/booking changes, DNS/email troubleshooting, integrations, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
