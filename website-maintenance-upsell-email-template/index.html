Do not make maintenance sound like a favor.

Many designers finish the launch, send a vague “let me know if you need anything,” and accidentally train the client to treat every small edit as included. A better closeout separates launch fixes, warranty support, and paid ongoing work.

The email below works best after the client has the live URL, final handoff packet, account/access inventory, and support boundary in writing.

What the email needs to do

Confirm the project is complete

Restate that the site is live, approved, and handed off so the care plan does not feel like unfinished project work.

Separate fixes from new work

Define launch-window fixes versus new requests, content edits, feature changes, updates, backups, and monitoring.

Offer a simple next step

Give the client one easy reply path instead of a menu of confusing packages.

Protect future scope

Put response times, included tasks, exclusions, and billing terms into a care-plan agreement before work starts.

Copy/paste maintenance upsell email

Subject: Ongoing website support after launch Hey [Client Name], Now that the website is live and the handoff packet is in your hands, I wanted to spell out the ongoing support options so there’s no confusion later. The launch support window covers anything that is broken from the launch itself, like a form not sending correctly, a redirect problem, a display issue we missed, or something that was approved but did not carry over properly. Separate from that, ongoing maintenance covers the stuff that happens after the project is complete: - plugin/theme/CMS updates - backups and basic monitoring - small content edits - new pages or sections - troubleshooting changes after launch - monthly cleanup and site checks - support requests from your team If you want me to keep handling that, I can put you on a simple care plan at [monthly price]. It includes [included items] with [response time] for support requests. Anything outside that scope gets quoted separately before I start. No pressure either way. If you would rather handle updates internally, that’s totally fine — the handoff packet has the access and notes you need. If you want me to send the care-plan agreement, reply “send it” and I’ll send the details over. Thanks, [Your Name]

Quick setup notes

  • ☐ Send this after the final handoff packet, not before the client has the basics.
  • ☐ Keep the offer simple: one recommended plan beats five confusing tiers.
  • ☐ Do not imply unlimited support. Include response time and exclusions.
  • ☐ Tie maintenance to concrete risks: updates, backups, small edits, broken forms, and monitoring.
  • ☐ If they say yes, send a written agreement before doing recurring work.
