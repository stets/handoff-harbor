DNS is not just a technical footnote.

The client does not need a networking lecture, but they do need a record of what keeps the site and email alive. A good handoff names the DNS host, exports the zone, labels what each record does, and makes future changes safer.

This is especially useful when a domain, hosting account, email provider, CDN, analytics tool, form provider, or verification record is owned by a different account.

What to document

Nameservers and DNS host

Record whether DNS lives at Cloudflare, the registrar, the web host, Webflow, Squarespace, Netlify, Vercel, or another provider.

Website records

Label the A, AAAA, and CNAME records that point the root domain, www domain, staging URLs, and redirects.

Email records

Copy MX, SPF, DKIM, DMARC, autodiscover, and any third-party mail routing records before launch changes.

Verification records

Save TXT/CNAME records for Google Search Console, analytics, payments, email platforms, SSL, CDN, and other services.

Copy/paste DNS records handoff checklist

  • ☐ Confirm the active nameservers and current DNS host.
  • ☐ Export the DNS zone or screenshot every record before making final changes.
  • ☐ Label A/AAAA/CNAME records for root, www, staging, CDN, redirects, and app subdomains.
  • ☐ Copy MX, SPF, DKIM, DMARC, autodiscover, and mail routing records.
  • ☐ Copy Google, analytics, payment, SSL, CDN, and platform verification TXT/CNAME records.
  • ☐ Note TTL values for records that may need quick launch-day changes.
  • ☐ Confirm who owns DNS access and who can approve future DNS changes.
  • ☐ Test site loading, SSL, email delivery, forms, checkout, analytics, and redirects after changes.
  • ☐ Add the DNS host, key records, and support boundary to the client handoff packet.
  • ☐ Tell the client not to delete unknown TXT/CNAME records without checking what they verify.

DNS handoff record template

DNS handoff record Website/domain: [example.com] Registrar: [provider] DNS host / nameservers: [provider / nameserver values] DNS account owner: [client / designer / agency / host] Who can approve changes: [name / role] Core website records: - Root domain: [A/AAAA/CNAME value] - www: [CNAME value] - Staging/dev: [record or none] - CDN/proxy: [Cloudflare / other / none] Email records: - MX: [values] - SPF: [value] - DKIM: [selector/value or location] - DMARC: [value] - Other mail records: [autodiscover / smtp / verification] Verification records: - Search Console: [record] - Analytics/tag manager: [record] - Payment/forms/email tools: [records] - SSL/CDN/platform records: [records] Support boundary: Future DNS edits, email troubleshooting, migrations, provider support calls, and broken third-party verification records are [included until date] / [covered by care plan] / [billable support].
