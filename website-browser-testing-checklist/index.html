Browser testing is not the same as “it worked on my laptop.”

Most handoff drama comes from obvious things nobody checked on the right device: sticky headers covering forms, hero text wrapping badly, buttons too close on mobile, Safari image issues, cookie banners hiding CTAs, or confirmation emails not arriving.

The handoff should show what was tested, where it passed, what devices/browsers were skipped, and which future browser bugs are covered by the launch support window versus billable changes.

What to test before client signoff

Core pages

Home, service/product pages, pricing, about, contact, policy pages, landing pages, and any campaign URLs the client will share after launch.

Devices and browsers

At minimum: Chrome desktop, Safari desktop or iOS Safari, Firefox desktop, a narrow mobile viewport, and one real phone when possible.

Revenue actions

Forms, booking links, checkout buttons, newsletter signup, payment embeds, quote requests, tracking events, confirmation pages, and notification emails.

Handoff notes

Record known issues, client-approved compromises, unsupported browsers, screenshots, test dates, and the support boundary for future fixes.

Copy/paste browser testing checklist

  • ☐ Home page, key service/product pages, contact page, and launch URLs load without console-visible breakage or missing assets.
  • ☐ Header, navigation, footer, CTAs, forms, embeds, and popups work in Chrome desktop.
  • ☐ Same key flow checked in Safari or iOS Safari, especially sticky sections, images, forms, and checkout/booking embeds.
  • ☐ Firefox desktop checked for layout, form, and interactive-section issues.
  • ☐ Mobile menu, tap targets, hero sections, forms, pricing tables, galleries, and accordions checked on a narrow viewport.
  • ☐ At least one real phone test completed if the site depends on mobile leads or booking.
  • ☐ Form submission, confirmation message, destination inbox/CRM, and notification email tested after final DNS/hosting setup.
  • ☐ Booking, checkout, payment, donation, or lead-magnet flow tested far enough to prove the client-owned account receives the action.
  • ☐ Accessibility basics checked: visible focus states, readable contrast, image alt text, labels for important form fields, and no keyboard traps.
  • ☐ Known browser quirks, skipped tests, client-approved tradeoffs, and post-launch support boundaries are listed in the handoff packet.
