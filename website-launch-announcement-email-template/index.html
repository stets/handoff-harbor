A launch announcement is part of the handoff.

A lot of web projects end with the designer saying “the site is live” and the client saying “great, now what do I tell people?” This small template gives them the next step: what is new, where to go, what action to take, and who to contact if something looks off.

Send this after final QA and launch approval, not before. The announcement should point to a site that is live, tested, and ready for real visitors.

What the announcement should include

Simple reason for the launch

Tell people what changed: clearer services, easier booking, new resources, improved shopping, better support, or a cleaner brand.

One primary link

Do not send five links. Pick the homepage, booking page, shop, services page, or announcement article.

Useful call to action

Invite readers to book, browse, reply, share, request a quote, download something, or update a saved link.

Feedback route

Give staff and close contacts a clear place to report typos, broken links, or launch-week issues without texting the designer directly.

Copy/paste website launch announcement email

Subject: Our new website is live Hi [Name/Team/Everyone], We just launched our new website: [website URL] The new site is designed to make it easier to [primary benefit: book an appointment / understand our services / browse products / get support / contact our team]. A few things you can do there now: - [Key action or page #1] - [Key action or page #2] - [Key action or page #3] If you have a minute, take a look here: [primary link] And if you notice anything that looks off, please send it to [feedback email/form] so we can clean it up quickly during launch week. Thanks, [Name]

Short social caption version

Our new website is live: [website URL] We rebuilt it to make [primary outcome] easier, with clearer information about [services/products/resources] and a better way to [book/contact/buy/get help]. Take a look here: [primary link]

Designer-to-client handoff note

Subject: Launch announcement copy for [Website Name] Hey [Client Name], The site is live and the core launch checks are done. Here is a simple announcement you can send to your list/team/social channels if you want to tell people about it: [paste announcement] Before posting, swap in your preferred link and make sure the call to action matches what you want visitors to do first. If anyone reports a typo or broken link during launch week, send those notes through [feedback route] so everything stays in one place. Thanks, [Your Name]

Pre-send checklist

  • [ ] Final QA is complete
  • [ ] Client has approved launch
  • [ ] Primary announcement link works on desktop and mobile
  • [ ] Contact forms, booking links, checkout, and analytics are tested
  • [ ] Feedback route is clear and does not create random text-message support
  • [ ] Client knows what is included during the launch support window
