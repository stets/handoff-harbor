Separate missing content from actual launch work.

A website can be technically ready and still impossible to finish because the client has not sent final copy, approved images, account access, or legal details. A content handoff checklist makes the missing pieces visible.

Send it before final QA, then use the answers to finish the site, prepare the handoff packet, and avoid surprise “one more thing” requests after approval.

What to collect before final handoff

Final page content

Approved headlines, body copy, service descriptions, FAQs, legal pages, pricing notes, and any content that should not be edited.

Media and brand assets

Final logos, favicon, team photos, product images, testimonials, alt text notes, brand colors, and usage restrictions.

Launch access

Domain registrar, DNS, hosting, CMS, analytics, form tools, email platform, payment platform, and plugin/license accounts.

Approval and ownership

Who signs off, who owns each account, what recurring costs exist, and which post-launch changes are outside the included support window.

Short email to send with the checklist

Subject: Final content checklist before launch Hey [Client Name], Before I finish the launch pass, I want to get all final content and account details in one place so nothing gets buried in old threads. Please work through this checklist and send anything still missing: [Paste checklist or link to shared doc] The main things I need are final page copy, approved images/files, account access for launch tools, and confirmation on who gives final approval. Once these are in, I can finish the final QA pass and package the handoff cleanly. Thanks, [Your Name]

Quick setup notes

  • ☐ Send the checklist before final QA, not the morning of launch.
  • ☐ Put responses in one shared doc, form, or project thread.
  • ☐ Mark missing content as a launch blocker, not a “designer delay.”
  • ☐ Keep new post-approval requests separate from final content fixes.
  • ☐ Move confirmed access, recurring costs, and ownership notes into the client handoff packet.
