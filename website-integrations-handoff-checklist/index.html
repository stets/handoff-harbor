Integrations are where clean launches get messy.

A website can look finished while half the business workflow still depends on somebody else’s account, credit card, API token, webhook, inbox, calendar, or automation. If that ownership is not written down, the client will come back weeks later asking why leads disappeared or payments stopped working.

The handoff should name each integration, what it does, who owns it, what account pays for it, how it was tested, and what happens when it needs changes later.

What to document before closing the project

Lead and sales flow

Record every form, payment link, checkout embed, calendar, newsletter signup, CRM destination, notification email, and confirmation message.

Accounts and billing

Confirm the client owns or can access each third-party account, billing owner, renewal date, plan limit, and admin/recovery email.

Automations and secrets

List Zapier/Make/n8n flows, webhooks, API keys, private tokens, environment variables, and where the client should rotate or store them.

Testing and support

Write down the last successful test, what was tested, known limitations, and which future integration changes are billable support.

Copy/paste integrations handoff checklist

  • ☐ All website forms are listed with destination inbox, CRM/list, required fields, spam protection, and confirmation behavior.
  • ☐ Payment buttons, checkout links, donation forms, booking tools, and embedded widgets are mapped to the correct client-owned account.
  • ☐ Newsletter, CRM, scheduling, chat, review, map, search, analytics, ad pixel, and social embeds are inventoried.
  • ☐ Zapier, Make, n8n, webhook, or custom automation owners are documented with trigger, action, failure notification, and last successful test.
  • ☐ API keys, private tokens, environment variables, and webhook signing secrets are not pasted into email; the client knows where they live and who can rotate them.
  • ☐ Plan limits, trial expirations, paid licenses, card-on-file owner, and renewal dates are recorded.
  • ☐ Test submissions, payments, booking confirmations, email notifications, CRM entries, and automation runs were checked before launch.
  • ☐ Known limitations, client-deferred integrations, missing account access, or unsupported tools are written down.
  • ☐ Future integration edits, broken third-party tools, API changes, deliverability fixes, payment/CRM changes, and automation troubleshooting are routed to warranty, care plan, or billable support.
