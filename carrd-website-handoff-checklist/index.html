A one-page Carrd site can still create a messy support tail.

The usual problems are boring but expensive: the client does not own the right account, billing stays on the freelancer's card, DNS is undocumented, forms go to the wrong inbox, embeds break quietly, mobile spacing gets changed after approval, and every small edit becomes a “quick favor.”

This is not legal, tax, or platform policy advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for freelancers and small studios so a Carrd launch ends with ownership, access, and support expectations in writing.

What to document before a Carrd project is accepted

Account, Pro plan, and billing

Confirm the client owns the Carrd account or has accepted transfer, knows the Pro plan level, renewal date, billing owner, recovery email, 2FA expectations, and your remaining access.

Domain, DNS, SSL, and launch settings

Record registrar ownership, connected domains, DNS records, SSL status, www/non-www preference, publish workflow, favicon, meta basics, and any redirect expectations.

Forms, embeds, analytics, and scripts

List form destinations, Zapier/Make/newsletter routes, payment or booking embeds, analytics, pixels, custom scripts, license/subscription owners, and renewal costs.

Editing and support boundaries

Separate included launch fixes from paid work: new sections, layout changes, mobile refinements, form automation, tracking, copy changes, integrations, and troubleshooting.

Copy/paste Carrd handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client controls or has accepted the correct Carrd account/site ownership, Pro plan, billing responsibility, recovery email, and 2FA expectations.
  • ☐ Remaining freelancer access, old contractors, shared logins, and unnecessary permissions have been reviewed and cleaned up.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, connected domains, DNS records, SSL status, www/non-www preference, email records, and renewal dates are documented.
  • ☐ Contact forms, notification recipients, newsletter/CRM routes, automations, spam handling, and test submissions are documented.
  • ☐ Embeds, payment links, booking widgets, analytics, pixels, Search Console, custom code, and third-party subscription owners are listed.
  • ☐ Mobile/tablet/desktop layouts, menus, buttons, forms, and high-priority conversion paths have been tested after final publish.
  • ☐ Client-safe editing notes explain what they can change without breaking spacing, sections, embeds, forms, or mobile layout.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, future recommendations, and unsupported requests are captured.
  • ☐ Included support window is stated, and new sections, design changes, mobile/layout revisions, forms, automations, SEO, integrations, or troubleshooting route to paid support.

Short Carrd handoff email

Subject: Carrd site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the Carrd site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the operating pieces are clear: account/site ownership, Pro plan billing, domain/DNS records, forms, embeds, analytics, mobile checks, editing notes, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New sections, layout/mobile changes, forms, automations, SEO work, integrations, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
