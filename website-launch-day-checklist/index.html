Do not hand off a site until launch has actually been checked.

A site can look approved in staging and still fail after DNS flips, redirects change, plugins update, forms send to the wrong address, or analytics never starts collecting. Launch day needs its own checklist.

This sits between your pre-launch QA pass and the final handoff packet: make it live, verify the critical paths, then tell the client exactly what changed and what happens next.

What to verify on launch day

Go-live basics

DNS, SSL, canonical domain, hosting environment, CMS settings, cache/CDN, backups, admin users, and rollback notes.

Critical paths

Forms, booking links, payment links, newsletter signup, phone/email links, navigation, search, and any high-value CTA.

SEO and tracking

Redirects, sitemap, robots, analytics, event tracking, Search Console, metadata, social preview, and noindex checks.

Client handoff

Final approval, live URL, handoff packet, access inventory, warranty/support window, maintenance offer, and first post-launch follow-up.

Handoff and support boundary [ ] Live URL sent to client [ ] Handoff packet prepared [ ] Account/access inventory updated [ ] Recurring costs documented [ ] Warranty/support window defined [ ] Post-launch changes separated from launch fixes [ ] Maintenance/care plan offer ready if relevant [ ] 30-day follow-up reminder scheduled

Short launch-day update email

Subject: Your website is live Hey [Client Name], The website is live here: [Live URL] I’ve completed the launch-day checks for the main pages, forms, redirects, SSL, tracking, and key links. I’m also packaging the final handoff notes so you have the important links, account details, support window, and next steps in one place. If you spot anything that looks broken from launch, send it over and I’ll review it under the launch support window. Any new requests or extra content changes after approval can go into a separate post-launch update list. I’ll send the full handoff packet next. Thanks, [Your Name]

Quick setup notes

  • ☐ Keep launch-day bugs separate from post-approval feature/content requests.
  • ☐ Verify the live domain, not just staging or a preview URL.
  • ☐ Test the client’s money paths first: forms, booking, checkout, phone, and email.
  • ☐ Send a launch update before the handoff packet so the client knows what has been checked.
  • ☐ Put access, recurring costs, and support boundaries into the final handoff packet.
