SEO handoff is risk management, not an upsell trick.

When search traffic drops after launch, clients rarely ask whether page titles, redirects, canonical tags, or Search Console ownership were documented. They ask the person who built the site to fix it for free.

A simple SEO handoff record makes the launch cleaner: what was checked, what changed, what is outside scope, and who owns future content, plugin, tracking, and indexing maintenance.

What to document before final signoff

Pages and metadata

Confirm every important page has a unique title, useful meta description, one clear H1, canonical URL, index/noindex status, and social preview where relevant.

Redirects and old URLs

Record old-to-new redirects, deleted pages, changed slugs, 404 risks, and who is responsible for adding redirects after future page changes.

Indexing and measurement

Verify sitemap, robots.txt, Search Console or Bing Webmaster Tools, GA4/analytics ownership, conversion events, and post-launch crawl/index checks.

Client responsibilities

List content-update expectations, local profile updates, blog ownership, plugin/app renewal notes, and when future SEO work becomes billable support.

Copy/paste SEO handoff checklist

  • ☐ Priority pages have unique title tags, meta descriptions, H1s, canonical URLs, and intentional index/noindex settings.
  • ☐ Old URLs, renamed slugs, deleted pages, and required redirects are documented and tested.
  • ☐ XML sitemap is live, linked where appropriate, and submitted or ready for submission in Search Console.
  • ☐ Robots.txt does not accidentally block the production site or important assets.
  • ☐ GA4/analytics, Search Console, Tag Manager, pixels, and conversion events are owned by the client or clearly documented for transfer.
  • ☐ Important forms, booking, checkout, and lead actions have tracking or at least a documented testing record.
  • ☐ Local business details, schema, Open Graph images, and social previews are checked for obvious launch mistakes.
  • ☐ Known SEO limitations are written down: missing copy, thin pages, duplicate content, slow assets, no blog plan, or client-owned decisions.
  • ☐ Post-launch indexing check date is scheduled, or the client knows it is part of a paid care plan.
  • ☐ Future SEO/content edits are explicitly outside the free support window unless included in the project scope.
