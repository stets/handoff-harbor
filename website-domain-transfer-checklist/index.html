Website domain transfer checklist

Domain handoff is where “the site is done” turns into broken email, dead forms, SSL errors, or a client locked out of their own registrar. Use this checklist before you move nameservers, transfer ownership, or hand a client the keys.

Do not transfer a domain like it is just another login.

The registrar account controls the client’s website, DNS, email, SSL, verification records, analytics ownership, and sometimes billing. Before anything moves, document who owns the domain, who manages DNS, what records exist, and what will break if nameservers change.

The goal is boring: the client owns the asset, the site stays online, email keeps working, and everyone knows who to contact next time the domain renews.

What to confirm before the move

Registrar ownership

Confirm the client controls the registrar account, billing email, recovery email, 2FA method, and renewal settings.

DNS authority

Identify whether DNS lives at the registrar, Cloudflare, hosting provider, Webflow, Squarespace, or another DNS host.

Email records

Copy MX, SPF, DKIM, DMARC, autodiscover, and any mail routing records before changing nameservers.

Launch dependencies

List SSL, redirects, forms, payment processors, analytics verification, Search Console, and third-party services tied to DNS.

Copy/paste domain transfer checklist

  • ☐ Confirm the domain name, current registrar, expiration date, auto-renew status, and billing owner.
  • ☐ Confirm who owns the registrar login and whether the client has working recovery access.
  • ☐ Export or screenshot the current DNS zone before changing anything.
  • ☐ Identify the active nameservers and where DNS should live after handoff.
  • ☐ Copy A, AAAA, CNAME, MX, TXT, SPF, DKIM, DMARC, verification, and redirect-related records.
  • ☐ Confirm email provider and test that mail delivery still works after DNS changes.
  • ☐ Check SSL status before and after the move.
  • ☐ Verify redirects, canonical domain, forms, checkout/payment flows, and tracking scripts after propagation.
  • ☐ Document the registrar, DNS host, renewal cost, renewal date, and support contact in the client handoff packet.
  • ☐ Tell the client exactly what you still manage and what they are responsible for after handoff.

Client handoff note for domain access

Domain: [example.com] Registrar: [Namecheap / GoDaddy / Cloudflare / other] DNS host: [Cloudflare / registrar DNS / hosting provider] Renewal date: [date] Auto-renew: [on/off] Billing owner: [client / agency] Email provider: [Google Workspace / Microsoft 365 / other] Important records: MX, SPF, DKIM, DMARC, verification TXT records, primary A/CNAME records What you own now: - The registrar account and domain billing - DNS access or delegated DNS permissions - Renewal responsibility unless a maintenance plan says otherwise What I still manage: - [ongoing support items, if any] Before changing DNS or transferring the domain again, save a copy of the current records and confirm email will not be interrupted.

Quick failure checks after transfer

  • ☐ Visit the www and non-www versions of the site.
  • ☐ Send a test form submission and confirm it arrives.
  • ☐ Send a test email to the client’s domain email.
  • ☐ Check SSL in a private browser window.
  • ☐ Confirm Search Console and analytics still verify.
  • ☐ Save final registrar/DNS notes in the handoff packet.
