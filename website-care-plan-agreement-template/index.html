The launch is the cleanest time to sell paid support.

The client just saw the work required to keep the site useful. Do not leave the next step vague. Give them a simple care plan agreement that says what you will monitor, update, fix, and advise on each month.

This page is not legal advice or a replacement for a lawyer-reviewed contract. It is practical copy you can adapt into your proposal, statement of work, or monthly support page.

What the agreement should cover

Included support

Updates, small content edits, backups, uptime checks, plugin/theme monitoring, analytics notes, and a monthly report if you provide one.

Excluded work

New pages, redesigns, custom features, third-party platform outages, emergency work, SEO campaigns, copywriting, and anything outside the agreed monthly scope.

Response times

Define normal business-hours response, urgent issue handling, and whether same-day work costs extra.

Billing and cancellation

State the monthly price, renewal date, payment method, minimum term if any, cancellation notice, and what happens after cancellation.

Copy/paste care plan agreement starter

Website care plan agreement Client/project name: Website URL: Care plan start date: Monthly fee: Billing date: Primary contact: Included each month: - Website software/plugin/theme update checks - Basic uptime and form-function checks - Backup verification, if backup access is available - Up to [X] minutes/hours of small content edits - Minor bug fixes tied to the existing site setup - Monthly summary email or report: [yes/no] Small edits include: - Text swaps on existing pages - Replacing images/files supplied by the client - Updating links, phone numbers, hours, team bios, or pricing text - Minor layout adjustments that do not require redesign Not included: - New pages, sections, funnels, templates, or redesign work - Custom development, integrations, automations, or advanced troubleshooting - Copywriting, SEO campaigns, ad landing pages, photography, or brand design - Third-party outages, hosting/email/DNS issues outside my control - Emergency work outside the agreed response window Response times: - Normal requests: reply within [X business days] - Urgent site-down/form-broken issues: reply within [X business hours] - Same-day or weekend work: billed separately unless included in the selected plan Client responsibilities: - Keep billing current - Provide accurate access when needed - Send requests through [form/email/helpdesk link] - Provide final copy, images, approvals, and business decisions - Notify me before making major site/account changes Billing and cancellation: - This care plan renews monthly until canceled. - Either party may cancel with [X days] written notice. - Unused monthly edit time does not roll over unless stated otherwise. - Work requested after cancellation is billed separately. Approval: By approving this care plan, the client understands what is included, what is excluded, and that larger work will be quoted separately before it begins.

Short email to offer it after launch

Subject: Optional monthly care plan for [Website Name] Hey [Client Name], Now that the site is live, the next thing to decide is who handles updates, small fixes, monitoring, and change requests going forward. If you want me to keep handling that, here is the monthly care plan option: [care plan link or short summary] It covers [brief included items] and gives you a clear place to send small website requests after launch. Bigger changes, new pages, or new features would still be quoted separately before I start them. No pressure either way — I just do not want ongoing support to be vague. Thanks, [Your Name]
