Use this before you say “project complete”

The dangerous part of a web design project is the fuzzy ending. If you launch without a closeout process, every missing login, unclear edit, DNS question, and “quick update” becomes your problem for free.

This checklist gives you a simple closeout sequence you can run before the final handoff email.

The closeout sequence

1. Finish final QA

Check responsive layouts, forms, links, browser basics, redirects, analytics, SEO titles, legal/footer details, and obvious content errors.

2. Capture approval

Get written signoff that the client reviewed the site and accepts known launch notes before final launch or final payment.

3. Collect payment

If the contract says final payment comes before launch or file transfer, do not soften it into “whenever works.” Say the rule plainly.

4. Package the handoff

Send one packet with links, account ownership, editing notes, recurring costs, support window, and next steps.

Copy/paste closeout checklist

Before final approval

  • [ ] Homepage, core pages, navigation, footer, and contact information reviewed
  • [ ] Mobile/tablet/desktop views checked on the important pages
  • [ ] Contact forms, booking links, checkout links, newsletter forms, and important CTAs tested
  • [ ] Legal pages, privacy links, cookie/banner notes, and footer details checked or assigned to client/legal owner
  • [ ] Analytics, pixels, Search Console, redirects, and launch-critical integrations confirmed or listed as exclusions

Before launch / transfer

  • [ ] Written launch approval received
  • [ ] Final invoice sent and payment rule stated clearly
  • [ ] Domain, hosting, CMS, plugin, font, stock, and third-party account ownership documented
  • [ ] Admin accounts and password-sharing method confirmed
  • [ ] Known issues, deferred requests, or phase-two items listed in writing

During handoff

  • [ ] Client handoff packet sent as one organized message or document
  • [ ] Included support window and bug definition explained
  • [ ] New-request boundary explained: extra content, design changes, pages, integrations, strategy, or ongoing edits
  • [ ] Maintenance plan or hourly support option offered
  • [ ] 30-day check-in or testimonial request scheduled

Short closeout email

Subject: Closing out [Website Name] Hey [Name], I’m wrapping up the closeout packet for [Website Name]. Before I mark the project complete, I’m checking three things: 1. Final approval is confirmed. 2. Final invoice / launch requirement is handled. 3. The handoff packet includes the links, access notes, support window, and next steps you’ll need. Once those are done, I’ll send the final handoff packet in one place so nothing gets buried across email threads. Quick note: the included support window covers launch bugs from the agreed scope. New content, design changes, extra pages, third-party setup, or ongoing edits can go through a maintenance plan or a new quote. Thanks, [Your Name]
