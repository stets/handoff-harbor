Ask after the client has felt the win.

The best time to ask is after the site is live, the handoff packet has been sent, and the client understands what changed. The worst time is months later when the project feels stale and they have to remember why they hired you.

Use the email below as part of your closeout or 30-day follow-up flow. It is short, specific, and easy for the client to answer.

What a good testimonial request includes

Context

Remind them what was delivered and why you are asking now. Do not send a generic “can you write me a testimonial?” note.

Prompts

Give 3–4 questions so they can reply in bullets. You can polish their wording only with permission.

Usage permission

Ask whether you may use their name, company, website link, or a shortened version on your site and proposal materials.

Low friction

Make a reply good enough. Do not force them into a form unless you already know they like forms.

Copy/paste testimonial request email

Subject: Quick favor now that [site/project] is live Hey [Client Name], Now that [website/project name] is live and the handoff is wrapped up, could I ask you for a short testimonial? A few bullets are totally fine. If it helps, you can answer any of these: - What was the main problem you wanted fixed before the project? - What felt better/easier after the new site launched? - What was the handoff or process like? - Would you recommend working with me to another business owner? If you reply with rough notes, I can turn them into a short draft and send it back for approval before using it anywhere. Also, are you okay with me using your [name/company name/website link] next to the testimonial, or would you prefer first name/company only? Thanks again, [Your Name]

Do this before you ask

  • [ ] Site is launched or final handoff has been sent
  • [ ] Client has received links, login/access notes, and support boundaries
  • [ ] Any urgent launch bugs are fixed or clearly scheduled
  • [ ] You know what name/company/link permission you want
  • [ ] You have a place to save the quote for your website, proposal deck, or case study

Optional shorter version

Subject: Could I use a quick quote? Hey [Client Name], Quick favor: now that the site is live, would you be willing to send 2–3 sentences about what changed after the project and what the handoff/process was like? Rough notes are fine. I can clean them up and send the final version back for approval before using it. Thanks, [Your Name]
