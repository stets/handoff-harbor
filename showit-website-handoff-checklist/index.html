A pretty Showit launch can still leave ugly loose ends.

The messy parts usually show up after final payment: the site is still under the designer account, the subscription is on the wrong card, DNS changes are undocumented, the blog is half-connected, forms route to the wrong inbox, mobile canvas edits break spacing, and the client treats every launch fix as included forever.

This is not legal, tax, or platform policy advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for designers and small studios so a Showit project ends with ownership, access, training, and support boundaries in writing.

What to document before a Showit project is accepted

Account, subscription, and roles

Confirm who owns the Showit account/site, subscription level, renewal date, billing owner, recovery email, collaborator access, and whether the client or studio keeps edit permissions.

Domain, DNS, SSL, and blog setup

Record registrar ownership, DNS records, SSL status, connected domains, WordPress blog connection, login route, hosting responsibilities, and any launch or propagation notes.

Forms, SEO, analytics, and integrations

List form destinations, email notifications, CRM/newsletter routes, analytics, pixels, Search Console, SEO titles/descriptions, tracking scripts, embeds, and subscription owners.

Training and support boundaries

Separate included launch fixes from paid work: new pages, canvas redesign, mobile layout changes, blog help, copy swaps, integrations, SEO work, and ongoing maintenance.

Copy/paste Showit handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client controls or has accepted the correct Showit site/account ownership, subscription level, billing responsibility, recovery email, and login process.
  • ☐ Freelancer/studio collaborator access, old contractors, shared logins, and unnecessary permissions have been reviewed and cleaned up.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, DNS records, SSL status, connected domains, www/non-www preference, email records, and renewal dates are documented.
  • ☐ WordPress blog connection, blog login URL, theme/template notes, post/category basics, backup responsibilities, and support ownership are documented.
  • ☐ Forms, notification recipients, CRM/newsletter automations, embeds, analytics, pixels, Search Console, and custom scripts are listed and tested.
  • ☐ Desktop and mobile layouts, navigation, buttons, forms, popups/embeds, and priority conversion paths have been checked after final publish.
  • ☐ Client editing notes explain what they can safely change without breaking mobile spacing, canvases, galleries, blog templates, forms, or scripts.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, future recommendations, and unsupported requests are captured.
  • ☐ Included support window is stated, and new pages, canvas redesigns, mobile/layout revisions, blog support, integrations, SEO, copy changes, or maintenance route to paid work.

Short Showit handoff email

Subject: Showit site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the Showit site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the operating pieces are clear: site/account ownership, subscription billing, domain/DNS records, the WordPress blog connection, forms, analytics, SEO basics, desktop/mobile editing notes, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New pages, canvas/layout changes, mobile revisions, blog help, integrations, SEO, copy changes, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
