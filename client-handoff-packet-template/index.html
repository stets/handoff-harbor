Client handoff packet template for website projects

A good website handoff packet gives the client one place to find the live site, admin links, ownership notes, renewal costs, editing instructions, support boundaries, and next steps.

If your current handoff is a launch email plus a few scattered links, clients will keep asking the same questions after the project should already be closed.

What the packet should include

  1. Project summary. What was delivered, what is live, and what changed from the original scope.
  2. Important links. Live site, admin login, analytics, forms, hosting, domain, and documentation.
  3. Ownership and renewals. Who owns each account, billing dates, renewal costs, and cancellation risk.
  4. Editing instructions. How to change common content without breaking the site.
  5. Post-launch support. What is included, how long it lasts, and what counts as paid new work.
  6. Maintenance option. The easiest way for the client to keep paying you for updates, backups, monitoring, and small fixes.

Copy/paste packet structure

# Website Handoff Packet Client: [Client Name] Project: [Website / Brand Name] Launch date: [Date] Prepared by: [Your Name] ## 1. Live website Live site: [URL] Admin/editing login: [URL] Staging site, if any: [URL] ## 2. Delivered items - [Homepage] - [Service pages] - [Contact form] - [Blog/CMS setup] - [Analytics/search setup] - [Other deliverables] ## 3. Accounts and ownership Domain registrar: [Provider + account owner] Hosting: [Provider + account owner] CMS/site builder: [Provider + account owner] Email/forms/tools: [Provider + account owner] Analytics/Search Console: [Provider + account owner] ## 4. Renewals and recurring costs - [Tool/provider]: [Cost] — renews [date] — paid by [person/company] - [Tool/provider]: [Cost] — renews [date] — paid by [person/company] ## 5. Editing notes Common edits the client can make: - [Edit text] - [Swap images] - [Publish blog posts] Please ask before changing: - navigation - page structure - forms/payments - plugins/integrations - DNS/domain settings ## 6. Support window Included post-launch support: [14/30] days for bugs related to approved launch scope. Not included: new pages, new features, copy rewrites, training, third-party tool changes, or ongoing maintenance. ## 7. Recommended maintenance Recommended plan: [Plan name] Includes: [updates/backups/monitoring/minor edits/reporting] Price: [Monthly price] Next step: [Payment/booking link]

Two details most freelancers forget

  • Renewal dates. Clients blame you when domains, hosting, plugins, or tools renew unexpectedly or expire.
  • Support boundaries. “Just email me if anything comes up” quietly turns into unpaid maintenance.

Simple delivery email

Subject: Website handoff packet Hi [Client Name], The website handoff packet is ready here: [Packet link] It includes the live/admin links, delivered items, account ownership notes, recurring costs, editing instructions, post-launch support details, and the recommended maintenance option. For the support window: I’ll cover bugs related to the approved launch scope for [14/30] days. New pages, new features, training, copy changes, or third-party tool work can be handled separately or through maintenance. If you want me to keep the site updated and handled after launch, I recommend [maintenance plan]. Thanks, [Your Name]
