Make the client describe the work before you price it.

A change request form is not bureaucracy. It keeps unpaid support from turning into a second project and gives you enough detail to say “covered,” “quoted separately,” or “maintenance plan item” without guessing.

Send this after launch with your support policy so clients know where requests go and what information you need.

Fields to include

Request basics

Client name, website URL, page URL, requested deadline, and the best contact for questions.

Request type

Bug fix, content edit, design change, new page/section, integration, training, or “not sure.”

Exact instructions

Ask for current text, replacement text, screenshots, assets, and where the change should appear.

Scope boundary

Include a checkbox that acknowledges new features, new pages, and post-warranty changes may be quoted separately.

Short email to send with the form

Subject: Best place to send website change requests Hey [Client Name], For any website changes after launch, please send them through this form: [form link] It helps me keep the request in one place and confirm whether it is covered in the post-launch support window or needs a separate quote. If it is a bug tied to the approved launch scope, I will handle it under the support policy. If it is new content, a new feature, a new page, or something outside the support window, I will send you the quote/next step before doing the work. Thanks, [Your Name]
