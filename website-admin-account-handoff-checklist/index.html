Access handoff is where messy projects stay messy.

A client can approve the design and pay the invoice while the actual ownership is still unclear. The admin account might be tied to your email, the client might not have recovery access, old collaborators might still be active, and nobody knows who owns 2FA.

The fix is not to dump passwords into a PDF. The fix is to create named accounts, assign roles, transfer recovery methods, remove temporary users, and document where access is managed.

What to confirm before final handoff

Owner account

The client has at least one owner/admin account under their email, not your agency email or a generic shared inbox you control.

Recovery and 2FA

Recovery email, phone, authenticator, backup codes, and billing contact point to the client or their chosen operator.

Role cleanup

Temporary developer, staging, VA, copywriter, plugin vendor, and test accounts are removed or downgraded.

Secure transfer

Passwords are shared through a password manager, account invite, or reset flow — not pasted into a handoff email.

Copy/paste website admin account handoff checklist

  • ☐ Create or confirm a named client owner/admin account.
  • ☐ Confirm the client can log in without using your email, phone, or authenticator.
  • ☐ Move recovery email, phone, backup codes, and security questions to the client where applicable.
  • ☐ Enable 2FA for owner/admin accounts and document who owns the second factor.
  • ☐ Remove temporary contractor, test, staging, and old employee accounts.
  • ☐ Downgrade your own account to the minimum access needed for the warranty window or care plan.
  • ☐ Confirm billing owner and renewal notifications for hosting, domain, CMS, plugins, forms, email, and analytics.
  • ☐ Store access notes in the client handoff packet without raw passwords.
  • ☐ Tell the client exactly how to request access help after launch and whether that help is paid support.
  • ☐ Save a dated screenshot/export of the final user list for your project archive.

Admin access handoff note

Admin access status Platform/CMS: [WordPress / Webflow / Shopify / Squarespace / custom / other] Login URL: [URL] Client owner account: [name/email] Recovery email/phone owner: [client/team] 2FA owner: [client/team] Billing owner: [client/team] Backup admin: [name/email or none] Designer/agency access after handoff: [removed / downgraded / kept for warranty / kept for care plan] Temporary accounts removed: - [developer] - [copywriter] - [testing] - [old vendor] Password transfer method: [password manager invite / reset link / client-created password / not applicable] Support boundary: After launch, access recovery help is [included through date] / [covered by care plan] / [billed separately]. Please keep your owner account and recovery method current.

Do this before you send the “we’re done” email

  • ☐ Log out and have the client confirm they can log in from their own account.
  • ☐ Check the final user list for old admins and forgotten vendor accounts.
  • ☐ Confirm the client knows where to update their password and 2FA.
  • ☐ Tie access help to your warranty or care-plan terms so it does not become endless free support.
