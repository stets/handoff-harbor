Website maintenance plan template for freelance designers

A clean handoff should not end with “let me know if you need anything.” Use the launch moment to offer a simple monthly maintenance plan with clear scope, boundaries, and next steps.

This page gives you a practical maintenance plan structure and copy you can adapt after a client website is approved, launched, or handed off.

The maintenance offer structure

  1. Name the outcome. Keep the site updated, monitored, backed up, and handled after launch.
  2. List included work. Updates, backups, uptime checks, security/plugin updates, small content edits, and monthly reporting if relevant.
  3. Define exclusions. New pages, redesigns, custom features, copywriting, SEO campaigns, ad hoc training, or third-party tool problems.
  4. Give one recommended tier. Do not make the client decode five options immediately after launch.
  5. Make the decision easy. Ask for a yes/no reply or link to a checkout/proposal.

Simple maintenance plan tiers

Care Plan — $149–$299/mo

  • Software/plugin/theme updates
  • Backups and uptime monitoring
  • Security checks
  • Up to 30 minutes of small content edits
  • Email support with a defined response window

Growth Plan — $399–$799/mo

  • Everything in Care Plan
  • 1–2 hours of monthly site improvements
  • Landing-page/content updates
  • Monthly recommendations
  • Priority response window

Copy/paste maintenance offer email

Subject: Keeping the site handled after launch Hi [Client Name], Now that the site is approved, I want to make the after-launch options clear. Your project includes [14/30] days of bug-fix support for issues related to the approved launch scope. That does not include new pages, new features, copy changes, training, third-party tool work, or ongoing updates. If you want me to keep the site maintained instead of handling it internally, I recommend the [Plan Name] plan: - updates/backups/monitoring - small content edits up to [time limit] - security/plugin checks - priority support for site questions - monthly notes on anything that needs attention Price: $[monthly price]/month If you want that active after launch, reply “maintenance” and I’ll send the short agreement/checkout link. Thanks, [Your Name]

Boundary language to include

The biggest mistake is selling “support” without defining it. Use plain language before the client needs help.

“Maintenance covers keeping the existing approved website healthy. New design work, new pages, new integrations, copywriting, SEO campaigns, or fixes caused by third-party account changes are quoted separately.”

When to pitch it

  • In the final handoff packet, while the client is thinking about ownership and next steps.
  • In the final invoice email, if payment and support boundaries are already being discussed.
  • At the end of the included support window, with a short “want me to keep handling this?” note.
