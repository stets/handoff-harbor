Hosting ownership is not obvious to clients.

A lot of website projects end with the client approving the design while the practical hosting details are still fuzzy. The account might be under the designer, renewal emails might go to the wrong inbox, backups might be assumed but not verified, and nobody knows whether emergency hosting support is included.

The fix is a boring handoff record: provider, owner, plan, billing, DNS, SSL, backups, staging, monitoring, and support boundaries in one place.

What to confirm before closing the project

Account ownership

Confirm whether hosting is owned by the client, your studio, or a managed provider, and document who can approve changes.

Billing and renewals

Record the hosting plan, billing owner, renewal date, payment method owner, and what happens if payment fails.

Backups and recovery

Verify backup frequency, retention, restore method, last successful backup, and who is responsible for testing restores.

Support boundary

Define whether hosting incidents, plugin conflicts, malware cleanup, migrations, and uptime monitoring are included, care-plan work, or billable support.

Copy/paste website hosting handoff checklist

  • ☐ Hosting provider, login URL, account owner, and support URL are documented.
  • ☐ Client has owner/admin access or understands that hosting remains managed by the designer/studio.
  • ☐ Hosting plan name, monthly/annual cost, renewal date, and billing owner are recorded.
  • ☐ DNS owner and nameserver location are documented separately from hosting.
  • ☐ SSL certificate status, renewal method, and expiration/auto-renew behavior are confirmed.
  • ☐ Backups are enabled, last backup date is checked, and restore responsibility is clear.
  • ☐ Staging site, dev site, or temporary build URLs are removed, protected, or documented.
  • ☐ Server/CMS/PHP/runtime versions and update responsibility are noted if relevant.
  • ☐ Uptime monitoring, malware scanning, cache/CDN, and performance tooling ownership is documented.
  • ☐ Post-launch hosting support is tied to the warranty window, care plan, or billable support process.
