Post-launch website checklist for freelance designers

Going live is not the finish line. The post-launch pass is where you catch broken forms, missing access, unclear support expectations, and all the little loose ends that become unpaid cleanup later.

Use this checklist after a client website is live, approved, or ready for final handoff.

The post-launch checklist

  1. Confirm the live URL. Open the production site in an incognito window and verify the final domain, SSL, redirects, favicon, and primary navigation.
  2. Test every conversion path. Submit contact forms, newsletter forms, booking links, checkout links, downloads, phone links, and email links.
  3. Check mobile basics. Review the homepage, highest-traffic pages, forms, menus, sticky elements, and footer on a phone-sized viewport.
  4. Verify analytics and pixels. Confirm analytics are installed, goals/events are firing if promised, and the client knows what is being tracked.
  5. Review SEO handoff items. Check titles, meta descriptions, indexability, sitemap, robots file, redirects, and social share previews.
  6. Lock down access. Remove temporary users, document admin accounts, hand over ownership, and note renewal dates for domains, hosting, plugins, apps, and email.
  7. Capture final proof. Save screenshots, QA notes, approved deliverables, launch date, and any known client-owned follow-up items.
  8. Send the handoff packet. Give the client one place with links, instructions, access notes, recurring costs, editing guidance, and support boundaries.
  9. Define the support window. State what is covered, what is excluded, where requests go, and when new work becomes a paid maintenance request.
  10. Offer the next step. Include a simple maintenance option so “can you just fix this?” does not become the default support model.

Copy/paste closeout note

“The site is live and I’ve completed the post-launch QA pass. I tested the main pages, forms, mobile layout, analytics, and handoff items. Your handoff packet is linked below with access notes, editing guidance, renewal items, and support details. Included bug-fix support runs through [date]. Anything outside the approved launch scope can be handled through maintenance or quoted separately.”

Common post-launch mistakes

  • Testing forms in staging but not on the live domain.
  • Leaving temporary admin access open after launch.
  • Forgetting to tell the client who owns each recurring bill.
  • Sending five separate emails instead of one organized handoff packet.
  • Waiting until after launch chaos starts to define support boundaries.

Where this fits in your workflow

Run this pass after launch and before you treat the project as complete. The best time to ask for final payment, testimonials, and maintenance is when the client can see that the site is live and the handoff is organized.
