Redirects are boring until the client loses leads.

Broken old URLs are one of the easiest launch problems to miss because the new homepage works, the client approves the design, and nobody clicks every old link from search, email campaigns, social bios, ads, PDFs, QR codes, or partner sites.

A redirect handoff turns that risk into a simple record: what moved, what was redirected, what intentionally died, what still needs monitoring, and what future URL changes cost after launch.

What to check before final signoff

Old URL inventory

Export URLs from the old sitemap, CMS, analytics top pages, Search Console, backlink tools, ad destinations, email templates, and any client-provided sales materials.

Redirect map

Map every important old URL to the closest relevant new page. Avoid redirecting everything to the homepage unless there is truly no equivalent replacement.

Launch-day tests

Spot-check priority redirects, confirm 301 status where possible, review common 404s, test trailing slashes/case variants, and verify canonical pages are reachable.

Ownership boundary

Document who owns future redirects, CMS slug changes, deleted pages, ad URL updates, Search Console review, and post-launch redirect cleanup.

Copy/paste redirects handoff checklist

  • ☐ Old sitemap, CMS page list, and top analytics/Search Console URLs were exported or reviewed.
  • ☐ Important old URLs are mapped to specific new destinations, not blindly sent to the homepage.
  • ☐ Ads, email links, social bio links, QR codes, PDF links, and partner links were checked where access exists.
  • ☐ Priority redirects were tested in a browser and/or status-code checker after launch.
  • ☐ Homepage, top service pages, contact page, booking page, and conversion pages return the expected status.
  • ☐ Known removed pages are listed with reason: merged, deleted, replaced, or client decision.
  • ☐ Search Console, analytics, and 404 monitoring ownership is documented.
  • ☐ Client knows that future page deletions, slug changes, campaign URLs, and redirect cleanup are paid support unless included in a care plan.
  • ☐ Redirect map and known 404 notes are saved inside the handoff packet.

Redirect map template

Website redirect handoff record Website: [client URL] Launch date: [date] Prepared by: [name] Priority redirect map: - Old URL: [https://oldsite.com/service] New URL: [https://newsite.com/services/service] Status tested: [301 / needs follow-up] Notes: [reason] - Old URL: [https://oldsite.com/blog/post] New URL: [https://newsite.com/resources/post] Status tested: [301 / needs follow-up] Notes: [reason] Known removed URLs: - [old URL] — [deleted / merged / no replacement / client approved] Launch-day checks: - Top pages tested: [yes/no + notes] - 404 review completed: [yes/no + notes] - Search Console/analytics owner: [client / agency / other] Support boundary: - Redirect fixes included until: [date] - Future slug changes, deleted pages, campaign URL updates, and SEO cleanup are: [care plan / quoted separately]
