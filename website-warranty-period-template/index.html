Use this before the final handoff email

The worst time to define a warranty is after the client has already found a vague issue. Put the terms in the handoff packet so everyone knows what is included after launch and what becomes a new request.

Keep it plain. The goal is not legal theater. The goal is fewer unpaid “quick fixes” and a cleaner path into a paid support plan.

What your warranty window should cover

Length of the window

State the exact number of days after launch or final delivery. Common windows are 14, 30, or 45 days depending on project size.

Included fixes

Limit included work to launch defects, broken links, responsive bugs, form errors, CMS setup mistakes, and issues caused by the delivered build.

Excluded work

Exclude new pages, copy changes, design changes, third-party outages, plugin changes, SEO requests, content loading, and anything altered by the client.

Request process

Name one support channel, expected response time, what screenshots/details to include, and what happens when the warranty period ends.

Copy/paste website warranty wording

Post-launch warranty period Your website includes a [14/30/45]-day post-launch warranty period beginning on [launch date / final delivery date]. During this window, I will fix bugs or launch issues caused by the delivered website build at no additional charge. Included warranty fixes may include: - Broken links or buttons from the approved build - Form delivery or configuration errors - Responsive layout issues on current major browsers - CMS setup mistakes from the handoff build - Plugin/theme configuration issues caused by the delivered setup The warranty does not include: - New pages, sections, copy, images, features, or design changes - SEO work, analytics interpretation, or marketing changes - Third-party outages, hosting issues, domain/DNS changes, or plugin/vendor changes outside my control - Issues caused by edits made after handoff by the client or another provider - Ongoing training, content entry, or general website support Please send warranty requests to [support email / project portal] with the page URL, a short description, screenshot or screen recording if possible, device/browser, and the result you expected. After the warranty period ends, new requests can be handled through a maintenance plan or quoted as separate work.

Quick checklist

  • [ ] Warranty start date and end date are written down
  • [ ] “Bug fix” is defined in normal client language
  • [ ] New scope and content changes are explicitly excluded
  • [ ] Client has one place to send requests
  • [ ] Response time is realistic, not instant-support cosplay
  • [ ] Maintenance or paid support option is offered before the warranty ends
  • [ ] Warranty language appears in the handoff packet, not just the contract
