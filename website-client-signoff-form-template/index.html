A launch approval is not the same as a vague “looks good.”

Freelance website projects get expensive when approval happens casually in a text thread. A client signoff form turns final review into a clear record: approved pages, accepted launch scope, known issues, payment status, support window, and change-request rules.

Use this before launch day or before handing over admin access.

What your signoff form should lock down

Final review scope

List the pages, forms, integrations, responsive checks, browser checks, and content areas the client reviewed.

Launch approval

Make the client explicitly approve launch or identify the exact blockers that must be fixed first.

Known issues

Separate true launch blockers from accepted limitations, future-phase requests, client-supplied content gaps, or third-party platform limits.

Payment and support boundary

State when final payment is due, what the warranty window covers, and how new requests become billable change requests.

Pre-launch signoff checklist

  • ☐ Send the client one final review link and deadline.
  • ☐ List every page and form included in final approval.
  • ☐ Record accepted known issues separately from launch blockers.
  • ☐ Tie approval to final invoice, launch timing, and handoff delivery.
  • ☐ Include the support window end date.
  • ☐ State that new requests after approval go through the change-request process.
  • ☐ Save the signed form in the project folder and link it from the handoff packet.

Boundary line to include

After this signoff is approved, requests outside the listed launch blockers will be handled through the change-request process or an active care plan. This keeps launch clean and prevents new work from delaying handoff and final payment.
