Final invoice website handoff template

The last invoice is where a lot of web projects get fuzzy. Tie final payment, ownership transfer, access, support boundaries, and maintenance into one calm handoff sequence.

This page gives you a simple closeout flow and copy you can adapt when a client site is approved, ready to launch, or already live.

The clean sequence

  1. Confirm approval. Make sure the client has signed off on the launch scope.
  2. Send the final invoice. Reference the contract/payment milestone without sounding defensive.
  3. Define what happens after payment. Access transfer, handoff packet, files, and admin invitations.
  4. Set the support window. Bugs inside approved scope are covered; new work is not.
  5. Offer maintenance. Give them an obvious way to keep paying you for real post-launch help.

Copy/paste final invoice handoff email

Subject: Final invoice + website handoff next steps Hi [Client Name], The website is approved and ready for final handoff. Final invoice: [Invoice link] Once payment is complete, I’ll send the full handoff packet with: - live site and admin/editing links - account ownership and renewal notes - delivered pages/assets - basic editing instructions - post-launch support details - recommended maintenance next steps For the post-launch support window, I’ll cover bugs related to the approved launch scope for [14/30] days. New pages, content updates, new features, training, or third-party tool requests can be handled separately or through a maintenance plan. If you want me to keep the site updated, backed up, monitored, and handled after launch, the maintenance option I recommend is: [Short maintenance offer] Once the invoice is paid, I’ll package everything and send the handoff packet in one place. Thanks, [Your Name]

If the site is already live

If you launched before collecting the final payment, keep the message direct. Do not threaten. Just make final payment the condition for the complete handoff packet, ownership notes, training links, or extended support.

Subject: Website is live — final handoff packet after invoice Hi [Client Name], The website is live here: [Live URL] The final invoice is here: [Invoice link] After payment clears, I’ll send the complete handoff packet with admin links, access notes, ownership/renewal details, support-window terms, and the recommended maintenance option.

What not to do

  • Do not send raw passwords in the final invoice email.
  • Do not use “let me know if you need anything” as your support policy.
  • Do not bury the maintenance offer three weeks later when the client has moved on.
  • Do not transfer every asset before the payment terms say you should.
