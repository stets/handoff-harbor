A Weebly site is not handed off just because the client has the login.

The messy version: the site lives under the wrong Square/Weebly account, the domain and plan renewal are unclear, contact forms route to an old inbox, store notifications are untested, apps are still tied to the freelancer, and every “quick edit” after launch becomes unpaid support.

This is not legal, tax, payment-processor, registrar, or Weebly/Square policy advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for freelancers and small studios so ownership, billing, operating notes, launch acceptance, and support boundaries are written down before final payment and maintenance conversations get awkward.

What to document before a Weebly project is accepted

Account, site, and plan ownership

Confirm the Square/Weebly account owner, site permissions, recovery email, 2FA method, paid plan, renewal date, payment method owner, and whether the freelancer should be removed after handoff.

Domain, SSL, email, and DNS

Record where the domain is registered, who controls DNS, whether SSL is active, what email service is used, what records exist, and who is responsible for renewal or troubleshooting.

Forms, store, apps, and notifications

Test form submissions, store checkout if used, pickup/shipping/tax basics, notification recipients, coupons, app connections, embedded widgets, newsletter/CRM links, and payment routing.

Editing notes and support boundary

Explain safe text/image/product edits, risky billing/domain/store/form settings, the included bug-fix window, excluded future work, and the path for paid maintenance or change requests.

Copy/paste Weebly website handoff checklist

  • ☐ Square/Weebly account owner, site users, recovery email, 2FA method, permissions, and freelancer removal plan are confirmed.
  • ☐ Paid plan, renewal date, payment method owner, invoices/receipts, domain renewal, SSL status, and cancellation risk are documented.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, DNS records, email provider, forwarding, redirects, and any third-party services connected to the domain are recorded.
  • ☐ Pages, navigation, mobile view, footer, contact details, business hours, legal/privacy pages, and accepted design tradeoffs are reviewed by the client.
  • ☐ Contact forms, lead routing, autoresponders, spam handling, store checkout, product settings, shipping/pickup/tax basics, and test submissions/orders are verified if used.
  • ☐ Apps, embeds, maps, calendars, reviews widgets, booking tools, newsletter/CRM integrations, pixels, analytics, and Search Console are listed with owner/login notes.
  • ☐ Client editing notes explain safe content/product/image edits, risky plan/domain/DNS/store/form/app settings, backup/export limits, and when to ask for help.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted limitations, future recommendations, and unsupported requests are written down before final signoff.
  • ☐ Included support window is stated, and new pages, copy changes, SEO, store changes, app setup, DNS/email troubleshooting, or ongoing maintenance route to paid work.

Short Weebly handoff email

Subject: Weebly site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the Weebly site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the operating pieces are clear: Square/Weebly account ownership, plan and domain renewals, DNS/SSL/email, forms, store settings if used, apps/integrations, analytics, editing notes, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New pages, copy edits, SEO work, store/app changes, DNS/email troubleshooting, integrations, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
