Do the boring check before the handoff call.

Clients remember the launch if the contact form breaks, the old URLs 404, or the mobile header covers half the screen. A simple pre-launch QA checklist protects the project margin and makes the final handoff feel professional.

Use this before sending the final invoice, approval form, handoff packet, or 30-day support window details.

What to check before launch

Device and browser pass

Home, key service pages, forms, navigation, menus, sticky elements, modals, and footers on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Forms and conversion paths

Contact forms, booking links, checkout links, newsletter forms, thank-you pages, notifications, spam protection, and required fields.

SEO and tracking

Titles, meta descriptions, canonical URLs, redirects, sitemap, robots, analytics, Search Console, event tracking, and social preview tags.

Ownership and recovery

Admin users, backups, DNS access, hosting access, licenses, plugin updates, domain renewals, and who owns each recurring account.

Short email to send before QA

Subject: Final QA pass before launch Hey [Client Name], I’m running the final QA pass now so we can launch cleanly and avoid loose ends after handoff. I’m checking the key pages, mobile layout, forms, tracking, redirects, access, backups, and the final approval items. If you have any final content corrections, please send them in one place by [date/time]. Anything new after final approval may need to go into a separate post-launch request so we can keep launch moving. Once QA is done, I’ll send the final handoff packet with links, access notes, support window details, and next steps. Thanks, [Your Name]

Quick setup notes

  • ☐ Run QA before the client handoff meeting, not during it.
  • ☐ Keep bugs, content changes, and new-scope requests in separate lists.
  • ☐ Screenshot anything that needs client approval or a future paid change.
  • ☐ Move DNS, hosting, analytics, and account ownership notes into the handoff packet.
  • ☐ Define the support window before the client starts sending “quick tweaks.”
