WordPress handoff is where vague support boundaries get expensive.

WordPress projects often involve hosting accounts, domain/DNS settings, premium plugins, theme licenses, SMTP, forms, analytics tags, security plugins, caching, backups, and update risk. If those pieces are not documented, the client does not know what they own — and you become the unpaid help desk.

This is not legal, cybersecurity, or accounting advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for freelance designers and small studios so the client understands ownership, maintenance responsibility, and what becomes paid support after launch.

What to document before a WordPress project is accepted

Admin and hosting ownership

Confirm the client controls WordPress admin, hosting, domain registrar, DNS, SSL, billing email, recovery email, and any agency-managed accounts being transferred.

Plugin/theme responsibility

List premium plugins, license owners, renewal dates, theme customizations, update risks, and which license costs are included or excluded after launch.

Operational testing

Document forms, SMTP/mail delivery, backups, restore point, caching, redirects, mobile checks, analytics, Search Console, and conversion or booking flows.

Support boundaries

Separate launch bugs from content edits, plugin updates, malware cleanup, hosting outages, new pages, SEO work, and ongoing maintenance.

Copy/paste WordPress handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client has owner-level WordPress admin access and recovery email is client-controlled.
  • ☐ Hosting account, billing owner, plan, server/PHP version, SSL status, and support contact are documented.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, DNS records, nameservers, email-related records, and renewal responsibility are documented.
  • ☐ Premium plugins, theme licenses, license owners, renewal dates, monthly/annual costs, and update limitations are listed.
  • ☐ Backups are configured, latest backup date is recorded, restore process/owner is stated, and offsite backup expectations are clear.
  • ☐ Security basics are covered: admin cleanup, 2FA recommendation, login protection, malware-scan owner, and emergency contact path.
  • ☐ Forms, SMTP/mail delivery, newsletter/CRM destinations, booking/payment embeds, and notification recipients are tested.
  • ☐ Cache/CDN/image optimization notes and known performance limits are documented.
  • ☐ Analytics, Search Console, pixels, tag manager, and conversion checks are owned or transferred to the client.
  • ☐ Content editing instructions, reusable blocks/templates, menu/footer editing notes, and page-builder warnings are included.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, future recommendations, and client signoff are recorded.
  • ☐ Included launch support window is stated, and maintenance/plugin updates/new pages route to a paid care plan or change request.

Short WordPress handoff email

Subject: WordPress site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the WordPress site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the important ownership and maintenance pieces are clear: WordPress admin access, hosting, domain/DNS, SSL, plugins, theme licenses, backups, forms, analytics, and update responsibility. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date]. New pages, content edits, plugin updates, hosting issues, malware cleanup, SEO work, or ongoing maintenance can be handled through a separate change request or care plan.
