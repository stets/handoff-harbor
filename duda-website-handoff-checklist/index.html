A Duda site can be live while the project is still not handed off.

The common failure mode is simple: the agency still owns too much, the client has the wrong permissions, billing renewals are unclear, DNS records are undocumented, forms point to an old inbox, app/widget settings are locked inside the builder, and every post-launch tweak becomes “just one more quick thing.”

This is not legal, tax, or Duda platform policy advice. It is a practical closeout checklist for agencies, freelancers, and small studios so a Duda project ends with ownership, access, acceptance, and support boundaries in writing.

What to document before a Duda project is accepted

Account, team, and client permissions

Confirm who owns the Duda account/site, which client portal or team users remain, what permissions they have, whether old contractors were removed, and who handles recovery/security.

Billing, publishing, domains, and DNS

Record the active plan, renewal owner, connected domains, DNS records, SSL status, www/non-www preference, redirects, and any launch propagation notes.

Forms, widgets, apps, and tracking

List every form destination, email notification, CRM/newsletter route, Duda widget/app integration, analytics script, pixel, Search Console setup, and custom code snippet.

Editing rules and support boundary

Show the client what they can safely edit, what can break layouts or dynamic content, what is included in launch support, and what becomes a paid change request or care plan.

Copy/paste Duda handoff checklist

  • ☐ Client account, site ownership, portal access, team roles, permissions, recovery email, and security owner are confirmed.
  • ☐ Agency/studio staff, old contractors, shared logins, and unnecessary user permissions have been reviewed and removed where appropriate.
  • ☐ Duda plan, renewal date, billing owner, invoice owner, included features, and any paid apps/widgets are documented.
  • ☐ Domain registrar, DNS records, SSL status, connected domains, redirects, www/non-www preference, and email-related records are captured.
  • ☐ Forms, notifications, CRM routes, newsletter integrations, booking tools, payment links, embeds, custom widgets, and app settings are tested and listed.
  • ☐ Analytics, pixels, Search Console, SEO titles/descriptions, schema/custom code, privacy/cookie tools, and conversion events are documented.
  • ☐ Backups, site duplication/export limits, version history expectations, and who can restore or roll back changes are explained.
  • ☐ Client editing notes explain safe edits, risky areas, page/template rules, global design settings, dynamic content, forms, widgets, and scripts.
  • ☐ Known issues, accepted tradeoffs, future recommendations, and unsupported requests are written down before final acceptance.
  • ☐ Included support window is stated, and new pages, layout changes, widget/app work, SEO, copy, integrations, troubleshooting, or ongoing maintenance route to paid work.

Short Duda handoff email

Subject: Duda site handoff + support boundaries Hey [Name] — the Duda site is ready for final handoff. Before we call the project accepted, I want to make sure the operating pieces are clear: site/account ownership, client permissions, plan billing, domains/DNS/SSL, forms, widgets/apps, analytics, SEO basics, editing notes, and the support window. Please review the handoff notes below by [date]. Launch bugs tied to the original scope are covered through [support end date].
