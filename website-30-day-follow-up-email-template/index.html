The post-launch email most designers forget to send

Thirty days after launch is when clients notice the tiny annoyances, forget how to edit things, and start asking for help again. Send one useful check-in before those requests turn into scattered unpaid support.

A good 30-day follow-up does three jobs.

It catches small launch bugs before the client gets annoyed, reminds them what is and is not included after handoff, and opens the door to paid maintenance without a hard sell.

Do not wait until the client sends a panicked message. Schedule this before the project closes.

Copy/paste 30-day follow-up email

Subject: Quick 30-day website check-in Hey [Client Name], It has been about a month since we launched [website/project name], so I wanted to check in and make sure everything still feels good on your side. A few quick questions: 1. Have you noticed anything broken, confusing, or not working the way you expected? 2. Are you comfortable making the routine edits we covered during handoff? 3. Are there any small updates you already know you want to make next? If there is a true launch-related bug, send it over and I will take a look under the warranty window we outlined in the handoff notes. If you want ongoing updates, backups, plugin checks, content changes, or monthly site support, I can also send over the maintenance options so you are not stuck handling everything yourself. Either way, I just wanted to make sure the handoff was clear and the site is behaving the way it should. Thanks, [Your Name]

What to include before you send it

Warranty end date

Know whether the client is still inside the bug-fix window. If they are outside it, change the wording before sending.

Maintenance path

Link or mention the simplest paid option for ongoing help. The email should not become a custom proposal from scratch.

Training reminder

Point them back to the handoff packet, Loom recording, or edit guide if the issue is actually “I forgot how to do this.”

Testimonial ask

If the client is happy, this is a clean moment to ask for a short testimonial or permission to use the project in your portfolio.

Optional testimonial add-on

If everything has been smooth so far, would you be open to sending a 2-3 sentence testimonial about the project? Nothing fancy — just what changed after the new site went live and what it was like working together.
