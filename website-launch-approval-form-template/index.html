Why launch approval matters

Launch week is where small web projects leak margin. The client says they are ready, then notices footer copy, missing team photos, DNS anxiety, tracking questions, and “one tiny edit” after the final invoice should already be paid.

A launch approval form gives you a clean record: what was reviewed, what is approved, who owns final content accuracy, and what support window starts after launch.

What to include in the approval form

Final review checklist

Pages, mobile views, forms, links, legal/footer content, analytics, and launch-critical integrations.

Content responsibility

State that the client approved spelling, pricing, service details, bios, addresses, phone numbers, and legal language.

Payment and launch rule

If your contract requires final payment before launch, say that plainly. Do not bury it inside a friendly email.

Post-launch boundary

Define the support window, what counts as a bug, what counts as a new request, and how maintenance is billed.

Copy/paste launch approval form

Website Launch Approval Project: [Client / Website Name] Prepared by: [Your Name / Studio] Date: [Date] Launch target: [Date or "after final payment clears"] By approving this launch, I confirm that I have reviewed the website and approve it for launch based on the items below. 1. Page and content review [ ] I reviewed the homepage. [ ] I reviewed the main service/product pages. [ ] I reviewed contact details, address, phone number, email, hours, pricing, bios, and any legal/footer content. [ ] I understand that future content changes after launch may be billed as new work unless covered by a support or maintenance plan. 2. Functionality review [ ] Forms were tested or approved for launch. [ ] Navigation links and important buttons were reviewed. [ ] Mobile and desktop views were reviewed. [ ] Any known limitations or remaining nice-to-have items are listed below. Known launch notes / exceptions: - [Item 1] - [Item 2] 3. Payment and access [ ] I understand final launch may depend on final invoice payment, account access, DNS access, or third-party approvals. [ ] I confirm the required launch access has been provided or assigned. 4. Post-launch support Included support window: [Example: 14 days for launch bugs] Bug definition: something included in the approved scope that is not working as agreed. New request definition: new content, new sections, design changes, feature additions, strategy changes, or third-party service work not included in the original scope. After the included support window, additional work will be handled through [maintenance plan / hourly rate / new quote]. Client approval Name: __________________________ Role: __________________________ Signature / typed approval: __________________________ Date: __________________________

Short email to send with it

Subject: Final launch approval for [Website Name] Hey [Name], I’m ready for final launch approval on [Website Name]. Before I push it live, please review the approval form here: [Link to form] Once that is approved and [final invoice / access / DNS] is handled, I can move into launch. Quick reminder: the included post-launch support window covers launch bugs from the agreed scope. New content, design changes, extra pages, third-party setup, or ongoing edits can go through a maintenance plan or a new quote. Thanks, [Your Name]
