The goal is not to sound pushy. The goal is to close the project.

A good final payment email tells the client exactly what is ready, what they need to approve, how to pay, and what happens after payment clears. It prevents the awkward limbo where the site is basically done but the handoff, credentials, and support expectations are still loose.

Use this when the website is ready for final approval and the remaining balance is due before full handoff.

What the email needs to include

Clear status

Say the site is ready for final review, not “almost done” or “let me know what you think.” Give the client a specific review link and deadline.

Final balance

State the remaining amount, link the invoice, and explain when payment is due without apologizing for normal business terms.

Handoff trigger

Connect payment to the next action: sending the handoff packet, transferring access, recording training, or scheduling the launch.

Support boundary

Briefly explain what is included after launch and how new requests or maintenance work will be handled.

Copy/paste final payment email

Subject: Final review + handoff for [website/project name] Hey [Client Name], The site is ready for final review here: [review link] Please look through the main pages, forms, mobile views, and any launch-critical details by [date]. If anything is off from the agreed scope, send it over in one list and I’ll clean it up before handoff. The remaining project balance is [amount]. You can pay the final invoice here: [invoice link] Once the final invoice is paid, I’ll send the full handoff packet with: - website/admin links - account and access notes - launch/support details - training or documentation links - next steps for maintenance or future updates After handoff, bug fixes tied to the original scope are covered through [warranty date]. New feature requests, design changes, extra pages, or ongoing updates can be handled through a maintenance plan or hourly support. Thanks, [Your Name]

Before you send it

  • [ ] Review link is correct and not password-blocked unless you included the password
  • [ ] Invoice link works and shows the correct amount
  • [ ] Final review deadline is specific
  • [ ] You listed what happens after payment clears
  • [ ] Handoff packet is ready or close enough to send quickly
  • [ ] Warranty/support boundary is written in plain language
  • [ ] Maintenance or hourly support option is mentioned without turning the email into a pitch deck
