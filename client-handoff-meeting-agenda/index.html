The handoff call decides whether the project actually closes.

Without an agenda, clients ask whatever pops into their head: tiny edits, passwords, invoice questions, future ideas, and support requests all mixed together. That makes the call feel helpful in the moment and expensive afterward.

A good handoff meeting separates launch review, training, access, support, and future work. The client leaves confident. You leave with a cleaner boundary.

What to cover in the handoff meeting

Launch status

Confirm the site is live, QA is complete, forms/checkouts are tested, and any known post-launch items are documented.

Client ownership

Review domains, hosting, CMS, analytics, forms, plugins, licenses, image accounts, and recurring charges.

Editing basics

Show the client how to make the few updates they are likely to own, not every possible admin setting.

Support boundary

State what is included during launch support, what counts as a change request, and how maintenance works.

Copy/paste handoff meeting agenda

Client Website Handoff Meeting Agenda Project: [Website / Client Name] Date: [Date] Attendees: [Names] Recording link: [Optional] 1. Confirm launch status — 5 minutes - Site URL: [URL] - Launch date/time: [Date] - QA status: [Complete / remaining notes] - Known follow-up items: [List] 2. Walk through the finished site — 10 minutes - Homepage - Main service/product pages - Contact/booking/checkout flow - Mobile view - Any pages the client will update often 3. Review account ownership and access — 10 minutes - Domain registrar: [Account owner] - Hosting/platform: [Account owner] - CMS/admin users: [Users] - Analytics/search tools: [Access] - Forms/email notifications: [Recipient] - Paid plugins/licenses/subscriptions: [Owner + renewal date] 4. Client editing training — 15 minutes - How to log in - How to edit common content - How to replace images safely - What not to touch without help - Where documentation/videos live 5. Support window and change requests — 10 minutes - Included launch support: [Days/weeks + what is included] - Not included: [New pages, design changes, new features, copy rewrites] - Change request route: [Form/email] - Response time: [Expectation] 6. Maintenance or care plan — 5 minutes - Recommended monthly/quarterly support: [Offer] - What it includes: [Backups, updates, small edits, reports, support] - How to start: [Link or reply] 7. Final next steps — 5 minutes - Client actions: [List] - Designer actions: [List] - Final invoice/payment status: [Status] - Follow-up date: [Date]

New pages, new features, larger design changes, or ongoing edits can go through [change request / care plan]. Thanks, [Your Name]
